Realtor vs. Lender vs. Escrow: Who Does What?

Buying a home in the United States involves several professionals working together. Understanding each role helps you stay informed, make better decisions, and avoid confusion during the transaction.

🔹 Realtor — Your Advocate and Negotiator

Your Realtor represents your interests throughout the entire process:

  • Searches for properties matching your criteria
  • Advises on fair pricing using market data (CMA)
  • Writes and negotiates your offer
  • Coordinates inspections, appraisals, and timelines
  • Guides you all the way through closing day

A good Realtor is your greatest asset in a competitive market.

🔹 Lender — Your Financing Partner

The lender is the bank or mortgage company that funds your home purchase:

  • Reviews your income, credit score, and financial profile
  • Issues pre-approval letters
  • Proposes loan options with interest rates and terms
  • Processes the final loan for closing

🔹 Escrow — The Neutral Third Party

Escrow acts as a trusted intermediary between buyer and seller:

  • Holds the earnest money deposit securely
  • Manages all documents and contracts
  • Ensures all conditions (inspections, repairs, loan approval) are met
  • Facilitates the final transfer of ownership

Why This Matters

When all three parties — Realtor, Lender, and Escrow — work in sync, your transaction is transparent, secure, and less likely to hit unexpected problems.
